I am a data scientist with broad experience in the collection, processing, visualisation and analysis of statistical data. Simply put: my passion is to discover knowledge from data. I am convinced that the potential of today's important data sources is far from fully leveraged. Hence, I focus on novel data forms (process- and user-generated data), modern retrieval techniques (web scraping, APIs), and cutting-edge analysis methods (machine learning, NLP).

I have completed a Master's degree in Social Sciences (M.A.) at Humboldt University of Berlin. From 2015 to 2016, I was a Global Visiting Scholar in the Applied Quantitative Research (M.A.) programme at New York University. In my master thesis, I analysed political communciation behaviour using an algorithm for the automated topical classification of Facebook posts.

In a addition to multiple years of being a freelance statistician (design and analysis of clinical trials, missing data), I have worked as a research assistant at the German Institute for Economic Research (DIW), the German Centre of Gerontology, and in various student assistant positions (both teaching and research) at the Department of Social Sciences at Humboldt University of Berlin. I have also been a research assistant to Prof. Ellen Immergut in the Norface project "HEALTHDOX - The Paradox of Health Care Futures" (funded by the German National Science Foundation) and to Prof. Spencer Henson at the Institute of Development Studies in London, UK.
