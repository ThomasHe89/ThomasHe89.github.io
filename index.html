I am a master student of Social Sciences (M.A.) at Humboldt University of Berlin. I also was a Global Visiting Scholar in the Applied Quantitative Research (M.A.) program at New York University (2015-2016) and a Visiting Student at the University of Sussex (2012).

I have worked as a research assistant at the German Institute for Economic Research (DIW), the German Centre of Gerontology, and in various student assistant positions (both teaching and research) at the Department of Social Sciences at Humboldt University of Berlin. I have also been a research assistant to Spencer Henson at the Institute of Development Studies in Brighton, UK and to Ellen Immergut in the Norface project "HEALTHDOX - The Paradox of Health Care Futures" (funded by the German National Science Foundation).

My main research interest lies in the intersection between data science, statistics, and the social sciences. I have broad experience in the collection, processing, visualisation, and analysis of survey data. In addition, I have provided assistance in weighting and missing data problems (especially multiple imputation) to two public research institutes. In my master thesis, I analyze political communciation behaviour on a large social media site using modern machine learning tools.

My preferred statistical programming languages are R, Stata, and Python. I also like creating interactive visualizations and maps using JavaScript.
