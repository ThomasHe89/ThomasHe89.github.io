I am a master student of Social Sciences (M.A.) at Humboldt University of Berlin. Last year, I was a Global Visiting Scholar in the Applied Quantitative Research (M.A.) program at New York University. I am a research assistant to Ellen Immergut in the Norface project "HEALTHDOX - The Paradox of Health Care Futures" (funded by the German National Science Foundation).

In the past, I have worked as a research assistant at the German Institute for Economic Research (DIW), the German Centre of Gerontology, and in various student assistant positions (both teaching and research) at the Department of Social Sciences at Humboldt University of Berlin. I have also been a research assistant to Spencer Henson at the Institute of Development Studies in Brighton, UK.

My main research interest is in party competition, political issue attention, and modern quantitative social science. I have applied text analysis tools to analyze parliamentary debates, newspapers, and online user-generated content. I have also provided assistance in weighting and missing data problems (especially multiple imputation) to two public research institutes.

My preferred statistical programming languages are R, Stata, and Python. In my free time, I like creating interactive visualizations and maps using JavaScript.
