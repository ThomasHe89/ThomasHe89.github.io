I am a master student of Social Sciences (M.A.) at Humboldt University of Berlin. From 2015 to 2016, I was a Global Visiting Scholar in the Applied Quantitative Research (M.A.) programme at New York University.

My main research interest lies in the intersection between data science, statistics, and the social sciences. I have broad experience in the collection, processing, visualisation, and analysis of statistical data. I have also provided assistance in weighting and missing data problems (especially multiple imputation) to two public research institutes. In my master thesis, I analyse political communciation behaviour on a large social media site using modern machine learning tools.

My preferred statistical programming languages are R, Python, and Stata. I also like creating interactive visualizations and maps using JavaScript.

In a addition to multiple years of being a freelance statistician, I have worked as a research assistant at the German Institute for Economic Research (DIW), the German Centre of Gerontology, and in various student assistant positions (both teaching and research) at the Department of Social Sciences at Humboldt University of Berlin. I have also been a research assistant to Prof. Spencer Henson at the Institute of Development Studies in Brighton, UK and to Prof. Ellen Immergut in the Norface project "HEALTHDOX - The Paradox of Health Care Futures" (funded by the German National Science Foundation).
